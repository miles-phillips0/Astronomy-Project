Venus

Sometimes called "Earth's twin" because of its proximity, size, and density, Venus is the second closest planet to the sun. Venus was named after the Roman Goddess of love and desire, but despite its beauty and surace-level similarity to the Earth, it is incredibly hostile to human life.

Venus boasts the record for being the hottest planet in our solar system (Unrelated to its namesake Goddess). Even though it isn't the closest planet to the Sun, Venus' thick and toxic atomosphere made up of carbon dioxide and sulfuric acid traps incoming heat. With a lead-melting surface tempurate of 900 degrees Farenheit, you definitely don't want to visit this planet without some serious insulating equipment.

Venus does have a magnetic field, but it is very weak. Comperable to Mercury's field, it is much weaker than every other planet's. Because of this, one would expect the planet to have a rough and battered surface. However, Venus has a very young surface. The surface is possibly as young as 150 million years old - very young for an ancient planet. This fact puzzles scientists. To this day, we still don't know how Venus resurfaced itself.

We have sent over 25 satellites to Venus. We have successfully completed many flyby, orbital, atmospheric, and landing visitors to this planet.
